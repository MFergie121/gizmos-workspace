# Domain, Why, and Who

## Domain
This visualisation explores **home-ground advantage in the Australian Football League (AFL)**. It focuses on whether AFL teams actually perform better at home, what factors might explain that advantage, and whether the Grand Final structure may favour clubs that are already familiar with the MCG.

## Why
Home-ground advantage is one of the most common assumptions in sport, but it is often discussed casually rather than tested carefully. In the AFL, possible explanations include travel burden, shared or unshared venue familiarity, environmental differences, and the long-standing use of the MCG for the Grand Final. This makes the topic a good candidate for data visualisation because the data can be used not just to confirm that an advantage exists, but to compare different explanations and show which ones are more convincing.

The visualisation is designed to answer a question that is meaningful to AFL followers and accessible to a general audience: **does home-ground advantage actually exist, and if it does, what appears to drive it?** The project ultimately argues that familiarity is a clearer explanation than travel or broad weather differences, and that this familiarity may become structural at Grand Final level.

## Who
The target audience is the **average Australian**, especially someone with a general awareness of AFL but not necessarily specialist statistical knowledge. The visualisation avoids technical language where possible and uses familiar chart forms, narrative titles, and plain-English explanations so that viewers can follow the argument without needing prior expertise in data analysis or sports analytics.
